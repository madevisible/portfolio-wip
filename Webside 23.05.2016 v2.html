About
Language is never just language.

I think synthetically, connecting patterns across systems, communication structures, research and lived experience into perspectives that are both analytically grounded and genuinely human. My work moves in two directions: I notice what is broken before users do, the cognitive load, the unclear hierarchy, the communication written from the inside out. And I notice what people already have but do not know they have, and build from there.

My background combines a Master's in Psychology of Language with hands-on experience in content design, information architecture and service analysis. I am most drawn to work where language genuinely determines outcomes: public-sector digital services, accessibility-critical products, neurodiversity-informed design and the thoughtful integration of AI into human-centred workflows. I also think in narrative systems, building worlds and ideas that hold their structural logic across different contexts and formats, from analysis documents to concept pitches to experiential design.

I am a mother of two children growing up bilingual in Reykjavík, which means I live the questions of metalinguistic awareness and cross-linguistic connection that appear in my work, not just as theory but as daily reality. I am based in Reykjavík and open to permanent or fixed-term employed positions, hybrid or remote.

Concept  ·  Product Thinking  ·  UX Writing  ·  2024
There Are Only Two Chairs
A concept for extending digital therapeutics to the people who hold daily life together when illness disrupts it
Digital health platforms are designed for patients. The informal caregiver, the partner, parent or sibling holding things together in the background, has no seat at the table. This concept builds the case for a parallel caregiver module, grounded in academic research and illustrated with microcopy that sounds like something a person would actually say.

How I Work
The thinking behind the writing.

I start from the human, not the brief

All four portfolio pieces began not with a commission but with a question: does this actually work for the person trying to use it? That instinct, noticing friction before it is named, is where my best work begins.

I work iteratively, not linearly

I rarely produce my best work in a single draft. I refine through cycles of reflection, restructuring and simplification. I often discover the real direction of a project while working on it. I follow that direction.

I build from what people already have

Not every communication problem is about what is missing. Sometimes the more useful question is what people already carry with them that has not been made visible yet. This applies to users navigating complex services, students approaching a new language, and worlds being translated across contexts and formats.

AI workflow: Perplexity for research and source verification, ChatGPT for visual iteration and execution, Claude for structure, editing and document production. Each tool has a defined role. The ideas, the direction and the judgement are mine throughout.
